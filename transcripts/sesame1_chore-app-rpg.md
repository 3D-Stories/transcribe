**Source:** sesame1.md (conversation with Sesame AI companion "Maya")

## Concept Summary

Combine Chris's existing chore app (already built with avatars, achievements, and monetary tracking) with a cooperative RPG/MMO-style game. Kids aged 6-16 complete real-world chores to unlock in-game progress — rebuilding a shared fantasy community threatened by monsters. Families and friends play on the same server, each household contributing to the world.

---

## Core Idea

- **Existing app features:** AI-generated avatars, achievement/goal system, dollar-figure tracking per chore
- **Target audience:** Kids aged 6-16, family households (Chris has 4 kids)
- **Problem being solved:** Engagement — all existing chore apps "fallen short"
- **Solution:** Gamification through a real RPG, not just badges and checklists

> [15:28] Chris: "I was trying to solve the engagement problem. We've used all of the other apps that are available for chores... and they've always kind of fallen short. So I wanted to build something that used a lot of gamification to solve that engagement problem."

---

## Game World Design

### Cooperative Community Rebuilding

- Sims-like family household expanded into a shared community
- Friends' families join the same server
- Each household contributes by completing real chores at home
- One family fixes roads, another rebuilds the school, another improves shops

> [19:10] Chris: "It's brewing an idea in my head where you take something like The Sims, where you have a family household but then expanded into the community, and you could have your friends' families be part of the same server and the kids in the families are building up the community by completing their chores at home."

> [20:09] "It's not just 'do this for me,' it's 'we're making this world better together.'"

### Fantasy Framing (Making Chores Feel Like Quests)

- Don't say "rebuild the school" — say "the school is protected by a magical barrier damaged during a monster attack"
- Each chore completed at home places a new barrier stone in the game world
- Doing dishes = actively defending a magical school from monsters

> [20:32] Maya: "It needs the right framing, not just rebuild the school, but maybe the school's protected by a magical barrier. Damaged during monster attack. Repair the barrier stones by completing real-world tasks at home."

### Progression System

- **Individual tasks** earn "magical fibers" (micro-rewards)
- **Achievements/milestones** (e.g., complete 10 chores) unlock full questlines
- Questlines escalate: repairing a local forest → defending the city center
- Natural escalation of game content as kids progress in real life

> [21:19] Chris: "I could see how like an individual task could earn some magical fibers, but a group of tasks or an achievement could really unlock a quest line."

---

## Platform Strategy

- **Start with iOS** — existing user base and tech already there
- Begin as an integrated mini-game within the chore app
- If traction proves the model, expand to a standalone game (possibly multi-platform)
- Avoid app sprawl — prove concept first, expand later

> [25:31] Maya: "Start with iOS. You mentioned the app exists there. Leverage that existing user base instead of building two separate foundations at once."

---

## Monetization

### In-Game Purchases with Chore Earnings

- Parents fund chore rewards as they already do
- Instead of cash payouts, kids spend earned money on in-game cosmetics/features
- Transaction flow goes through the app's system
- Kids feel they earned it; parents feel it's going somewhere productive

> [27:19] Chris: "What about something like the kids could use their earned money to purchase additional in-game items and that's a way to monetize the system."

### Additional Revenue Streams

- **Premium avatars/customization** — families pay for deluxe looks
- **Subscription model** — access to exclusive seasonal storylines/events (battle pass style)
- **Brand partnerships** — sponsors fund in-game buildings or items

### Dual Currency System

- Game currency (gold/fibers) earned through tasks for in-game progress
- Real dollar amount visible underneath avatar for real-world rewards
- Both motivations running simultaneously
